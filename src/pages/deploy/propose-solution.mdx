## Start the conversation

When proposing a solution to your client, you must invite the relevant stakeholders and potential sponsors to this meeting. As part of this practice, you will need to understand who in the client team will be able to help you and become a strong sponsor. You can use the <a href='https://ibm.seismic.com/Link/Content/DCQjf8FMD4jpb89XB624MmTQ9h48'>Relationship Barometer</a> in ISC to help you gauge the political power of the client team. You will want to provide a briefing on the solution’s capabilities and how those capabilities map to the client’s strategic priorities.<br/>

Use the <Link to='/onboard/business-outcomes-framework'>Business Outcomes Framework</Link> to describe the value - the savings, the process improvement, the time reduction - that the customer anticipates as a result of implementing a solution. Focus on the business value that this solution drives and remember that the client has competing priorities for time and resources and you will want to demonstrate a compelling reason to act.<br/>
The Business Outcomes Framework is a collection of IBM Customer Success best practices, tools, and templates for guiding the definition and measurement of the business value clients expect from an implemented solution. Use the calculators and KPIs to guide your conversation with the client.

## Outcomes of this stage

The outcome of this stage is that a sponsor and <Link to='onboard/stakeholder-map' target='_blank' rel='noreferrer noopener'>stakeholders</Link> are interested in the solution and want to move forward. This might lead to sponsorship of the project or the client might want to see a demo or a Proof of Value (PoV).

A PoV is a build activity led by IBM Client Engineering (CE) that demonstrates value of the IBM product based on a client’s use case or technology pattern. The PoV is hands-on with the client or the client’s input. It showcases how a solution can meet the success criteria for a specific use case. For more information on a Proof of Experience (PoX) that can be delivered, see the <a href='https://pages.github.ibm.com/skol/ce-valueengineering-docs/#/pox' target='_blank' rel='noreferrer noopener'>Client Engineering playbook</a>.

To request that CE work an opportunity that you have identified, you can enter the <a href="https://ibmsc.lightning.force.com/lightning/page/home" target='_blank' rel='noreferrer noopener'>opportunity in ISC</a>. You might have a situation where CE has already done a Proof of Experience (PoX). If so, meet with the CE team to learn what opportunity was being addressed with the technology pattern that was implemented.
