Companies today are modernizing <a href="https://www.ibm.com/cloud/learn/customer-care" target='_blank' rel='noreferrer noopener'>Customer Care</a>, using advanced AI to ensure a positive customer experience starting from the first interaction and throughout the buyer's journey.

It's about personalizing the customer experience through analytics and automated interaction so that service can take place 24x7 tailored to the individual. When we refer to "customer" they can be either customers in the literal sense as those that purchase a company's goods or services, citizen constituents from various levels of government, or a company or organization's own employees.

How can IBM clients accelerate their journey in being able to provide exceptional care that delights their customers? IBM has identified three patterns that clients can quickly deploy with watsonx Assistant: Customer Self Service, Agent Assist, and Contract Center Insights.

## ![use-case](../images/use-case-14.svg) Customer self service use case

With this pattern, AI powers conversations between an enterprise and its customers in natural language, both written and spoken. For that to happen, our customers can’t rely on chatbots powered by a simple rules engine. This is why IBM developed watsonx Assistant, the top enterprise AI virtual assistant in the market, which ensures that customers who seek help don’t hit frustrating dead ends. Using advanced natural language understanding (NLU) and AI-powered search, watsonx Assistant dynamically taps into a broad and constantly growing set of relevant information to find the most accurate answer. And if that still does not resolve a customer’s need, watsonx Assistant can pass the conversation over to a human agent — including the conversation history and context (for example if AI models were run for a sentiment score or churn risk) so the customer doesn’t have to start the contact cycle from the beginning all over again.

## ![use-case](../images/use-case-14.svg) Extending across Data & AI use case

A perfect customer experience is not delivered by improving individual customer service touch-points. Rather, it’s a continuous feedback loop which improves every interaction a customer has with the company. This is accomplished by knowing your customer, and that’s made possible by ensuring that all available data points are integrated into the customer care experience.

**Data Fabric**

* Update Data Sets with Customer Interactions
* Enrich Personalization with enterprise data 


**Business Analytics**

* Drive new personalized products and services from analytic decisions
* Feed business analyses with data sets from customer interactions


**Data Management**

* Store, Manage, and integrate existing data sets with new data captured from solutions above to unlock value and apply
