## What is zSecure and what does it do? <br/>

The IBM zSecure Suite adds layers of security assurance — including audits, alerts, administration, reporting and authentication — that enhance the security and risk management in IBM Z hardware, software, virtualization, and standard external security managers (ESMs) such as IBM RACF, CA ACF2, and CA Top Secret.

The zSecure Suite automates security administrative tasks to help increase efficiency and reduce errors, detects internal and external threats, issues real-time alerts, and monitors compliance.

The zSecure Suite includes:

- ***User authorization products:*** Secure the core of your business with a user authentication strategy that accommodates all functions and privilege.
  - [**IBM Z Multi-Factor Authentication:**](https://www.ibm.com/products/ibm-multifactor-authentication-for-zos) Secure strong authentication to strengthen user controls with integration for easy administration.
  - [**zSecure Command Verifier:**](https://www.ibm.com/products/command-verifier) Enforce mainframe compliance to policies through granular controls for RACF commands.
  - [**zSecure Audit:**](https://www.ibm.com/products/zsecure-audit) Detect and report security events and exposures on mainframes for ESMs.
  - [**zSecure Alert:**](https://www.ibm.com/products/zsecure-alert) Reduce the overhead of security risk management by monitoring the mainframe for external and internal security threats, including alerts.
- ***Compliance automation products:*** Automate and simplify IBM security and compliance administration tasks while enhancing RACF delegation capabilities and identity governance.
  - [**zSecure Admin:**](https://www.ibm.com/products/zsecure-admin) Adds a user-friendly layer over RACF to help improve administration and reporting.
  - [**zSecure Visual:**](https://www.ibm.com/products/zsecure-visual) Save costs by decentralizing RACF administration through a Microsoft Windows-based GUI.
  - [**zSecure CICS Toolkit:**](https://www.ibm.com/products/zsecure-cics-toolkit) Free RACF resources from routine administrative tasks through a CICS interface.
  - [**zSecure Manager for RACF z/VM:**](https://www.ibm.com/products/manager-for-racf-zvm) Combine audit and administration functionality for the VM environment.

## What are the pain points that zSecure addresses?

**Pain Point:** Increased threat of attacks on the mainframe from external sources. ***IBM zSecure Alert*** resolves this by providing real-time mainframe threat monitoring of intruders and alerting to identify misconfigurations that could hamper compliance.

**Pain Point:** Modernization initiatives have increased vulnerability. ***IBM zSecure Audit*** handles this by vulnerability analysis for the mainframe infrastructure. It automatically analyze and report on security events and monitor compliance.

**Pain Point:** Struggle having enough trained, efficient mainframe staff, much less having security experts. ***IBM zSecure Visual*** and ***IBM zSecure CICS Toolkit*** allows easy access and flexibility. IBM zSecure Visual helps reduce the need for scarce, Resource Access Control Facility (RACF)-trained expertise through a Microsoft Windows–based GUI for RACF administration where as IBM zSecure CICS Toolkit provides access to RACF command and APIs from a CICS environment, allowing additional administrative flexibility.

## The steps to deploy and who is responsible for what, when?

**Identify a pain point, sponsor and use case**<br/>
The IBM team of Sales, Tech Sales and potentially CSM can use the list of pain points above or a Client Engineering engagement to identify use cases and sponsors.<br/><br/>
**Demonstrate how a given pain point can be addressed (demo) in the use case**<br/> Help your customers understand <a href="https://ibm.seismic.com/Link/Content/DC6W8PcCCqcgmGWHHjhpbgHmP8TP" target='_blank' rel='noreferrer noopener'>the value of IBM Security zSecure Suite</a>.  This deck makes it really clear what capabilities are unique to the IBM Security zSecure suite and what is available in it. Also, make use of the <a href="https://techzone.ibm.com/collection/security-live-demos-collection#tab-1" target='_blank' rel='noreferrer noopener'>Tech Zone - Security Live Demos Collection</a>.<br/><br/>
**Prove value (proof of experience (PoX)) of the use case**<br/>
Tech Sales, Client Engineering, and CSM can work together to connect the product to the customers' systems and prove the capabilities resolve the pain points.<br/><br/>
**Implement a project**<br/>
CSMs can work with the customer to determine how the customer would like to implement their project. Initially, there should be a conversation between Security Technology Expert Labs, yourself, and the client to scope the opportunity and engage our most qualified team. The customer may elect to utilize an implementation partner and/or do the work themselves. Regardless of how the deployment is conducted, ensure that you are involved in the process, keeping close contact with the teams and maintaining a proactive relationship with the client. Please pay close attention to the  <a href='https://w3.ibm.com/w3publisher/pps-shortcuts/sel-offerings' target='_blank' rel='noreferrer noopener'>SEL Offerings</a> as a guideline for any deployment conversations with the client.

## There isn't a current use case, where do I start?

Most customers are somewhere on their security journey and if you talk to the right people you will find the pain points that are listed above.

As a CSM, drive to at least gain approval from the customer to run a discovery workshop to identify and prioritize use cases and potential solution designs. Do this with the participation of IT and LOB in the room or MURAL session at the same time. If you need assistance running a workshop reach out the Practice Leaders, Greg Sabatini or Hemankita Perabathini for assistance. You can also ask to have Client Engineering, or Security Technology Expert Labs.
